Category 1: General Child Protection
Question: What is the main law for child protection in Sri Lanka?

Response: The National Child Protection Authority (NCPA) Act is the main law. In 2026, Sri Lanka also introduced the Child Rights Act to make sure all children are safe and treated fairly according to international standards.

Question: Is it legal to hit a child in school or at home?

Response: No. Under the new Penal Code (Amendment) Act, corporal punishment (hitting or physical discipline) is strictly prohibited in schools, homes, and childcare centers.

Question: At what age can a child start working?

Response: The legal age to start working in Sri Lanka is 16 years old. Children under 18 are never allowed to do dangerous work.

Category 2: Reporting and Emergency Help
Question: How can I report child abuse in Sri Lanka?

Response: You can call the 1929 ChildLine. It is a free, 24-hour hotline that supports Sinhala, Tamil, and English. You can also visit the Children & Women Bureau at your local police station.

Question: Who is responsible for reporting child abuse?

Response: While everyone should report it, professionals like teachers, doctors, and police officers are legally required to report any suspicion of child abuse to the NCPA.

Category 3: Education and Online Safety
Question: Is school mandatory in Sri Lanka?

Response: Yes. Every child in Sri Lanka must attend school until they reach the age of 16.

Question: Are there laws for online safety for children?

Response: Yes. Sri Lanka has updated cybercrime laws that punish people for "online grooming," sharing private photos of children, or bullying minors on the internet.

Category 4: Safety for Organizations (Hiring)
Question: What are the rules for hiring people to work with children?

Response: Organizations must follow strict vetting rules. As of 2026, anyone working with children must pass a fingerprint-based criminal record check through the NCPA to ensure they have no history of child abuse.

Question: What happens to state employees who abuse children?

Response: Sri Lanka has a "Zero Tolerance" policy. Any state employee convicted of child abuse is banned for life from working in any government institution.
